Publication and Ethics Statement

ICEPHSS’25: Evolving Perspectives in Humanities and Social Sciences is committed to upholding the highest standards of academic integrity and ethical publishing practices. This commitment applies to all aspects of the conference, including the submission, review, and publication of scholarly work.

Key Principles:

  • Transparency and Integrity: All submissions must be original works and free from plagiarism or unethical practices. Authors are required to appropriately credit all sources, collaborators, and funding agencies.

Peer Review Process:

  • Submissions will undergo a rigorous double-blind peer review to ensure academic rigor, impartiality, and quality.
  • Reviewers will evaluate papers based on relevance, originality, methodology, and contribution to the field.

Conflict of Interest:

Authors, reviewers, and editors must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to ensure unbiased evaluation and decision-making.

Copyright and Open Access:

Published works will be subject to copyright agreements with the publisher while adhering to open-access principles (if applicable). Authors will retain certain rights, ensuring broad dissemination of their research.

Ethical Responsibilities of Authors:

  • Authors must confirm that their work complies with ethical research guidelines, including obtaining necessary approvals for studies involving human or animal subjects.
  • Simultaneous submission to multiple venues or redundant publication is strictly prohibited.

Ethical Responsibilities of Reviewers:

  • Reviewers must maintain confidentiality and objectivity in evaluating submissions.
  • Constructive feedback should be provided to authors without prejudice or bias.

Plagiarism and Misconduct:

The conference uses plagiarism detection software to identify and address cases of academic misconduct. Proven instances of plagiarism, fabrication, or falsification will result in the rejection of the manuscript and reporting to relevant authorities.

Commitment to Diversity and Inclusion:

The conference and its publications promote diversity, equity, and inclusion by welcoming contributions from scholars worldwide, irrespective of gender, ethnicity, or institutional affiliation. This statement reflects the conference’s dedication to fostering a culture of academic excellence and ethical scholarship.

Plagiarism Policy

ICEPHSS’25 strictly enforces a plagiarism policy to maintain the integrity and originality of the research presented at the conference.

Plagiarism Threshold:

  • All submitted papers must have a plagiarism ratio below 15%, as assessed using reliable plagiarism detection tools such as Turnitin or iThenticate. Similarity from a single source must not exceed 5%.

Scope of Plagiarism:

  • Plagiarism includes verbatim copying, paraphrasing without proper citation, and using ideas or data from other works without acknowledgment.
  • Self-plagiarism (reusing one's previously published work without proper citation) is also prohibited.

Verification Process:

All submissions will be screened for plagiarism during the initial review phase. Manuscripts exceeding the acceptable similarity index will be returned to the authors for revision or rejected outright.

Consequences of Violations:

Papers found to have significant plagiarism will be immediately disqualified. Authors engaging in academic misconduct may face further actions, such as being barred from future conferences or reported to their institutions.

Author Responsibility:

Authors are responsible for ensuring the originality of their work and appropriately citing all sources and collaborators. This policy underscores the commitment of ICEPHSS’25 to fostering a culture of ethical and credible research.
