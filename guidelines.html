Submission Guidelines

Authors are encouraged to carefully read the following guidelines before submitting their papers to ICEPHSS’25. These instructions are intended to ensure a smooth submission, review, and publication process.

Formatting Requirements:

    Papers should be written in English and formatted in A4 size (PDF or DOC format).

  • Use Times New Roman, 12-point font, with 1.5 line spacing.

  • Maximum word count: 6,000 words (including references).

  • Include a structured abstract (max. 250 words) with keywords.

Review Process:

All submissions to ICEPHSS’25 will undergo a rigorous double-blind peer review process to ensure the highest standards of academic rigor, impartiality, and quality. During this process:

  • Reviewers will evaluate papers based on relevance, originality, methodology, and contribution to the field.

  • Both the authors' and reviewers' identities will remain confidential.

  • Feedback will be provided to help authors improve their work, even if the submission is not accepted.

Publication and Ethics Statement:

ICEPHSS’25 is committed to upholding the highest standards of academic integrity and ethical publishing practices. This commitment applies to all aspects of the conference, including submission, review, and publication. The key principles include:

  • Transparency and Integrity: All submissions must be original works, free from plagiarism or unethical practices, and appropriately credit all sources and collaborators.

  • Conflict of Interest: Authors, reviewers, and editors must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to ensure unbiased evaluation and decision-making.

  • Copyright and Open Access: Published works will be subject to copyright agreements with the publisher, while adhering to open-access principles where applicable.

  • Ethical Research Practices: Authors must confirm that their work complies with ethical guidelines, including obtaining necessary approvals for studies involving human or animal subjects.

Plagiarism Policy:

ICEPHSS’25 strictly enforces a plagiarism policy to maintain the integrity and originality of the research presented.

  • Plagiarism Threshold: All submitted papers must have a similarity index below 15%, with no more than 5% similarity from a single source, as assessed using tools like Turnitin or iThenticate.

  • Scope of Plagiarism: Plagiarism includes verbatim copying, paraphrasing without proper citation, and using ideas or data from other works without acknowledgment. Self-plagiarism is also prohibited.

  • Verification Process: Submissions will be screened during the initial review phase. Manuscripts exceeding acceptable similarity levels will be returned for revision or rejected outright.

  • Consequences of Violations: Significant plagiarism will result in disqualification. Authors found engaging in academic misconduct may face further actions, including being barred from future conferences or reported to their institutions.

  • Author Responsibility: Authors are responsible for ensuring the originality of their work and appropriately citing all sources and collaborators. This policy underscores the commitment of ICEPHSS’25 to fostering a culture of ethical and credible research..

Commitment to Diversity and Inclusion:

ICEPHSS’25 is dedicated to fostering a culture of academic excellence, diversity, equity, and inclusion. The conference welcomes contributions from scholars worldwide, irrespective of gender, ethnicity, or institutional affiliation.
